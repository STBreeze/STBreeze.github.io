Project Overview

This project is the final capstone for the IBM Data Science Professional Certificate. The goal is to predict whether the Falcon 9 first stage will land successfully, which is crucial for reducing launch costs and enabling reusability. The project involves data collection, analysis, machine learning, and interactive visualizations.

üåç The Challenge: Making Spaceflight Affordable

Space exploration is expensive‚Äîreally expensive. Traditional rockets are single-use, meaning each mission comes with a massive price tag. Enter SpaceX. By designing the Falcon 9 first stage to be reusable, they've slashed launch costs from $165 million to just $62 million. But here's the catch: landing a booster isn't guaranteed. Successful landings depend on multiple factors:

  • Payload mass (how heavy is the cargo?)
  • Orbit type (where is the rocket headed?)
  • Launch site conditions

üí° Can we predict whether a Falcon 9 booster will land successfully? That's exactly what this project sets out to do.

üìä The Data Behind the Mission

To build accurate predictions, I worked with multiple datasets:

  • API Data: Directly pulled from SpaceX's official launch records.
  • Web Scraped Data: Extracted historical launch records from Wikipedia.
  • Geospatial Data: Mapped the exact GPS locations of launch sites.
  • SQL Database: Used SQL queries to analyze trends and mission outcomes.
  • Machine Learning Dataset: Engineered key features like payload mass, orbit type, and flight history.

Every dataset played a critical role in shaping the final predictions.
